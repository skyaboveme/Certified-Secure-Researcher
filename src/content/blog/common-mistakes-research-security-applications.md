## Category 1: Disclosure Errors
Disclosure mistakes are the most common‚Äîand often most serious‚Äîapplication errors. Here's what to watch for:
<div class="mistake-card critical">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity">Critical</span>
<h3>Incomplete Foreign Activity Disclosure</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Omitting foreign appointments, visiting positions, or collaborations that don't involve direct payment.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Researchers often assume that only paid positions need to be disclosed, or they forget about informal arrangements made years ago.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Disclose ALL foreign affiliations, regardless of compensation</li>
<li>Include honorary positions, visiting appointments, and advisory roles</li>
<li>Review your CV and ORCiD record before completing disclosures</li>
<li>When in doubt, disclose‚Äîreviewers can determine relevance</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="mistake-card critical">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity">Critical</span>
<h3>Underreporting Current and Pending Support</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Failing to include all sources of research support, especially in-kind contributions, equipment access, or indirect funding.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Many researchers don't recognize certain forms of support as requiring disclosure, particularly when no money changes hands directly.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Include in-kind support: equipment, lab space, personnel, materials</li>
<li>Report support from all sources: government, industry, foundations, foreign entities</li>
<li>List pending applications, not just awarded funding</li>
<li>Include internal institutional support and seed grants</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="mistake-card moderate">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity moderate">Moderate</span>
<h3>Inconsistent Information Across Forms</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Providing different dates, amounts, or descriptions for the same activity on different disclosure forms.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Researchers complete multiple forms at different times without cross-referencing, or they estimate when exact information is available.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Maintain a master document with all disclosure information</li>
<li>Use exact figures from official sources, not estimates</li>
<li>Review all submitted forms together before finalizing</li>
<li>Update all forms simultaneously when information changes</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="mistake-card moderate">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity moderate">Moderate</span>
<h3>Outdated Time Commitment Information</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Reporting time commitments that don't add up correctly or that don't reflect actual current activities.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Project responsibilities evolve over time, and researchers don't update their commitment percentages to reflect changes.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Verify total commitments don't exceed 100% (or justified amount)</li>
<li>Review and update commitments at least quarterly</li>
<li>Account for teaching, administrative duties, and other obligations</li>
<li>Be realistic about actual time spent on each activity</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
## Category 2: Documentation Failures
Even accurate information can cause problems if not properly documented:
<div class="mistake-card moderate">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity moderate">Moderate</span>
<h3>Missing or Expired Supporting Documents</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Submitting applications without required attachments or with outdated versions of supporting documents.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Requirements change over time, and researchers use old templates or forget that certain documents need annual renewal.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Review current requirements before each submission</li>
<li>Check document dates and expiration requirements</li>
<li>Maintain organized digital files of all supporting documents</li>
<li>Set calendar reminders for document renewals</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="mistake-card minor">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity minor">Minor</span>
<h3>Incorrect File Formats or Naming</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Submitting documents in wrong formats (e.g., Word instead of PDF) or with non-compliant file names.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Applicants don't read formatting requirements carefully or assume any format is acceptable.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Read all formatting and naming conventions before preparing documents</li>
<li>Use required naming formats exactly as specified</li>
<li>Convert documents to required formats before submission</li>
<li>Test file accessibility after conversion</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="mistake-card moderate">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity moderate">Moderate</span>
<h3>Inadequate Training Documentation</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Unable to provide evidence of completed security training or providing certificates that don't meet requirements.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Training completions aren't tracked systematically, or researchers complete training that doesn't satisfy specific requirements.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Save all training certificates immediately upon completion</li>
<li>Verify training meets specific agency or program requirements</li>
<li>Use centralized tracking systems like CSR certification</li>
<li>Confirm training provider is recognized and approved</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
## Category 3: ORCiD-Related Issues
As ORCiD becomes mandatory for research security applications, related errors are increasingly common:
<div class="mistake-card moderate">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity moderate">Moderate</span>
<h3>Incomplete ORCiD Profile</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Linking an ORCiD iD that lacks current employment, education history, or recent works.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Researchers create ORCiD accounts but don't maintain them, or they don't understand that reviewers will check the profile.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Update your ORCiD profile before each application</li>
<li>Ensure current and past employment is listed</li>
<li>Link recent publications and grants</li>
<li>Set up automatic imports from publisher databases</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="mistake-card minor">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity minor">Minor</span>
<h3>Privacy Settings Blocking Verification</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Setting ORCiD profile to private, preventing reviewers from verifying disclosed information.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Researchers set restrictive privacy settings without realizing the impact on verification processes.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Set profile visibility to at least "trusted parties"</li>
<li>Make employment and education publicly visible</li>
<li>Understand the difference between public, limited, and private settings</li>
<li>Review settings specifically before application submission</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="mistake-card critical">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity">Critical</span>
<h3>ORCiD Doesn't Match Application Information</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>ORCiD profile shows affiliations or works not disclosed in the application, or vice versa.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>ORCiD profile and application are updated at different times, or information was added to ORCiD but forgotten on applications.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Cross-reference ORCiD profile against application disclosures</li>
<li>Explain any apparent discrepancies proactively</li>
<li>Update both ORCiD and applications simultaneously</li>
<li>Use ORCiD as your master record for accuracy</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
## Category 4: Process and Timing Errors
Sometimes the information is correct, but process mistakes cause problems:
<div class="mistake-card moderate">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity moderate">Moderate</span>
<h3>Missing Deadlines for Updates</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Failing to update disclosures when circumstances change during the application or certification period.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Researchers don't realize that disclosure requirements are ongoing, not just at application time.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Understand update requirements and timelines</li>
<li>Set reminders for required update periods</li>
<li>Report material changes promptly (typically within 30 days)</li>
<li>Don't wait for renewal to report new affiliations or support</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="mistake-card minor">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity minor">Minor</span>
<h3>Submitting Too Close to Deadlines</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Waiting until the last minute to submit, leaving no time for corrections if issues are found.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Researchers underestimate application complexity or have competing priorities.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Start applications at least 4-6 weeks before deadlines</li>
<li>Build in buffer time for unexpected issues</li>
<li>Complete applications in stages, not all at once</li>
<li>Have a colleague review before submission</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="mistake-card moderate">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity moderate">Moderate</span>
<h3>Ignoring Requests for Information</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Not responding promptly to requests for clarification or additional documentation.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Emails go to spam, researchers are traveling, or they procrastinate on responding.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Whitelist email addresses from certifying bodies</li>
<li>Check spam folders regularly during application periods</li>
<li>Respond within 48-72 hours to all requests</li>
<li>Designate a backup contact if you'll be unavailable</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
## Category 5: Institutional Coordination Failures
Individual researchers can do everything right but still face issues due to institutional problems:
<div class="mistake-card moderate">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity moderate">Moderate</span>
<h3>Conflicting Information from Institution</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Information provided by the researcher conflicts with institutional records or certifications.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Institutional systems aren't updated, or researcher and institution use different definitions or categories.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Verify information with your grants office before submission</li>
<li>Ensure institutional systems have current information</li>
<li>Understand how your institution categorizes activities</li>
<li>Coordinate with compliance office early in the process</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="mistake-card minor">
<div class="mistake-header">
<span class="mistake-severity minor">Minor</span>
<h3>Missing Required Institutional Signatures</h3>
</div>
<div class="mistake-body">
<div class="mistake-example">
<strong>The Mistake:</strong>
<p>Submitting applications that require institutional authorization without obtaining proper signatures.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-why">
<strong>Why It Happens:</strong>
<p>Researchers don't realize institutional sign-off is required, or they don't allow time for the signature process.</p>
</div>
<div class="mistake-fix">
<strong>How to Avoid It:</strong>
<ul>
<li>Identify all required signatures early in the process</li>
<li>Allow 1-2 weeks for institutional review and signature</li>
<li>Know your institution's internal deadlines</li>
<li>Build relationships with grants administration staff</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>

## What to Do If You've Made a Mistake
Discovered an error after submission? Here's how to handle it:
<div class="recovery-steps">
<div class="recovery-step">
<div class="step-number">1</div>
<div class="step-content">
<h4>Don't Panic</h4>
<p>Most errors can be corrected. Proactive disclosure of mistakes is always better than waiting for them to be discovered.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="recovery-step">
<div class="step-number">2</div>
<div class="step-content">
<h4>Contact Immediately</h4>
<p>Reach out to the certifying body or grants office as soon as you identify the error. Explain what happened clearly and completely.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="recovery-step">
<div class="step-number">3</div>
<div class="step-content">
<h4>Provide Corrected Information</h4>
<p>Submit accurate information promptly. Include an explanation of the discrepancy if relevant.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="recovery-step">
<div class="step-number">4</div>
<div class="step-content">
<h4>Document Everything</h4>
<p>Keep records of all communications about the correction. This protects you if questions arise later.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="recovery-step">
<div class="step-number">5</div>
<div class="step-content">
<h4>Learn and Improve</h4>
<p>Update your personal processes to prevent the same mistake from recurring on future applications.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="warning-box">
<h4>Important Warning</h4>
<p>Never attempt to hide or minimize errors, especially disclosure omissions. Intentional misrepresentation has far more serious consequences than honest mistakes. When in doubt, over-disclose and explain‚Äîreviewers appreciate transparency.</p>
</div>
