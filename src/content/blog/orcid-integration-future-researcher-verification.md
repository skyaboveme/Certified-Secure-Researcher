<h2>Addressing Common Concerns</h2>
<p>Despite its benefits, ORCiD integration raises legitimate questions. Here's how leading institutions are addressing them:</p>
<div class="concern-list">
<div class="concern-item">
<div class="concern-question">
<span class="q-icon">Q</span>
<h4>What about researcher privacy?</h4>
</div>
<div class="concern-answer">
<p>ORCiD is designed with privacy as a core principle. Researchers control their own records and can set visibility levels for each piece of information. Privacy settings can limit what's visible to the public, trusted parties, or only the researcher themselves. Institutions should work with researchers to explain these controls and respect individual privacy preferences.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="concern-item">
<div class="concern-question">
<span class="q-icon">Q</span>
<h4>How do we handle researchers without ORCiDs?</h4>
</div>
<div class="concern-answer">
<p>Registration is free and takes minutes. Most institutions now require ORCiD registration as part of onboarding. For legacy researchers, provide clear instructions and support for registration. Consider making ORCiD a requirement for grant submissions to drive adoption.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="concern-item">
<div class="concern-question">
<span class="q-icon">Q</span>
<h4>What if ORCiD records are incomplete?</h4>
</div>
<div class="concern-answer">
<p>ORCiD records are only as complete as researchers make them. Institutions should encourage (and potentially require) maintenance of accurate, complete records. Integration with institutional systems can automatically update ORCiD records when publications or grants are recorded internally.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="concern-item">
<div class="concern-question">
<span class="q-icon">Q</span>
<h4>How do we verify ORCiD record accuracy?</h4>
</div>
<div class="concern-answer">
<p>ORCiD includes verification mechanisms‚Äîitems added by trusted organizations show as "verified." Cross-referencing with other databases (Crossref, DataCite, institutional records) helps identify discrepancies. Automated verification workflows can flag potential issues for human review.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
