<section>
<h2>The Five Blind Spots Costing Research Organizations Millions</h2>
<div class="blind-spot-card">
<div class="blind-spot-number">1</div>
<div class="blind-spot-content">
<h3>The Trusted Insider Paradox</h3>
<p>Your greatest asset‚Äîyour research team‚Äîrepresents your biggest vulnerability. We've remediated over <strong>4,000 insider risks</strong>, and the pattern is clear: organizations consistently underestimate the threat from within. It's not about malicious intent; it's about:</p>
<ul>
<li>Researchers who don't understand the value of what they're sharing</li>
<li>Well-meaning collaborators who inadvertently expose critical data</li>
<li>Former team members who retain access long after departure</li>
<li>Graduate students transitioning to competitor organizations</li>
</ul>
<div class="blind-spot-insight">
<strong>The blind spot:</strong> Most institutions focus on external threats while their IP walks out the front door every day.
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="blind-spot-card">
<div class="blind-spot-number">2</div>
<div class="blind-spot-content">
<h3>The Collaboration Trap</h3>
<p>International partnerships accelerate innovation, but they also create complex security challenges. When you're collaborating with institutions across different regulatory environments, who's responsible for protecting the IP? How do you maintain security when research teams span multiple countries with varying security standards?</p>
<div class="blind-spot-insight">
<strong>The blind spot:</strong> Assuming your security policies extend to your partners' environments. They don't.
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="blind-spot-card">
<div class="blind-spot-number">3</div>
<div class="blind-spot-content">
<h3>The Technology Transfer Time Bomb</h3>
<p>The gap between research completion and patent filing is where millions in IP value disappears. During this critical window, unprotected innovations are vulnerable to theft, premature disclosure, or competitive exploitation. We've seen groundbreaking research lose its commercial value because security protocols didn't account for the technology transfer process.</p>
<div class="blind-spot-insight">
<strong>The blind spot:</strong> Treating research security and commercialization as separate concerns instead of integrated processes.
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="blind-spot-card">
<div class="blind-spot-number">4</div>
<div class="blind-spot-content">
<h3>The Digital Footprint You Can't See</h3>
<p>Modern research generates massive digital footprints across cloud platforms, collaboration tools, and personal devices. Each researcher might use a dozen different platforms to store, share, and analyze data. Meanwhile, your security team monitors maybe three of them.</p>
<div class="blind-spot-insight">
<strong>The blind spot:</strong> Believing that official channels are the only channels. Researchers will find ways to collaborate‚Äîthe question is whether you'll secure those pathways or remain blind to them.
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="blind-spot-card">
<div class="blind-spot-number">5</div>
<div class="blind-spot-content">
<h3>The Compliance Checkbox Mentality</h3>
<p>Meeting minimum compliance requirements doesn't equal security. We've assessed organizations with perfect compliance scores that were hemorrhaging IP through unmonitored channels. Compliance is about checking boxes; security is about protecting value.</p>
<div class="blind-spot-insight">
<strong>The blind spot:</strong> Confusing regulatory compliance with comprehensive risk management.
</div>
</div>
